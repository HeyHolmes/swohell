SWO Survival Guide

For other NYC homeowners going through this. Everything we learned the hard way.

Process Flowchart
  1. 🚨Violation issued (DOB inspection)
  2. 🛑Stop Work Order (partial or full)
  3. 📞Hire expediter
  4. 🔧Complete corrective work
  5. 📄File Certificate of Correction (COC) with AEU
  6. 💰Pay civil penalties
  7. ⏳COC reviewed by AEU (4–5 weeks)Or go in person to 280 Broadway (owner only, deed + ID)
  8. ✅COC approved → violation resolved
  9. 📨Submit docs to OBM for SWO rescission
  10. 📅OBM schedules rescission inspection
  11. 🔍DOB inspector visits → SWO lifted
  12. 🏗️Construction resumes

Key insight: COC approval and SWO rescission may be INDEPENDENT tracks. You might not need the COC approved before the SWO can be lifted. Confirm with OBM.

10 Things I Learned
01
Your expediter can’t do everything. Two out of three AEU reps told me only the owner can go to 280 Broadway in person to speed up a COC review. Bring your deed and ID. Don’t assume your expediter is handling it — verify.
02
Call multiple times. I called AEU three times and got different info each time. One rep said my expediter could come. Two said only the owner. Triangulate.
03
COC and SWO may be separate tracks. AEU reviews your COC. OBM handles the SWO. One rep told me they don’t think COC approval is needed before the SWO can be lifted. Push both simultaneously.
04
The 60-day deadline may be for filing, not approval. Neither AEU nor OBM flagged our April 18 date as a crisis. The COC was filed before the deadline. The May 6 ECB hearing is where penalties get adjudicated.
05
DOB gets frustrated with too many follow-ups. My contractor warned me: expediters can push to a point, then DOB pushes back. Balance persistence with patience.
06
Check DOB NOW yourself. Don’t wait for your expediter. Go to the DOB NOW portal and look up your property. I found my civil penalty review was approved before anyone told me.
07
Go to 280 Broadway. If your COC review is taking too long, go in person. 280 Broadway, lower Manhattan, 8:30–3:45 weekdays. Deed + government ID. Highest-impact move an owner can make.
08
Document everything. Every call, text, email. I built this entire site because it’s the only way to track what’s happening across 10+ people and 3 government agencies.
09
Know AEU vs. OBM vs. ECB. AEU reviews your Certificate of Correction. OBM handles SWO rescission and inspections. ECB handles hearings and penalty adjudication. They’re separate.
10
Jack Jaffa is an expeditor, not a law firm. Despite the name, Jack Jaffa & Associates handles DOB paperwork. Legal defense is separate.
